The Loxley Experience

We Don't Offer Amenities.
We Offer Chapters.

Your stay at Loxley is a story with a beginning, a middle, and a moment you'll still be talking about five years from now.

Chapter One

The Crossing

Your first steps across the hundred-foot rope bridge. The ground drops away. The trees rise to meet you. Your hands grip the ropes. Your heart beats faster. And then, halfway across, something happens — you stop noticing the height and start noticing the silence.

Part of every stay. It's not optional — it's the way home.

Chapter Two

First Light

The canopy at sunrise isn't a view. It's a slow-motion event. The mist lifts off the valley. The first birds call. The leaves above you glow gold, then green, then gold again. You're watching it from your bed, sixty feet in the air.

Part of every stay. Set an alarm. You won't regret it.

Chapter Three

Fire & Flour

Fresh dough and our Gozney stone oven are waiting for you on the deck — bring your favorite toppings and channel your inner Top Chef 60 feet above the forest floor.

Part of every Loxley stay.

Chapter Four

After Dark

Champagne, chocolate-dipped strawberries, a blanket, and a seasonal star chart. The treehouse goes quiet. The canopy opens up. And you realize you've never actually seen the sky before.

Available exclusively for guests during their stay.

Chapter Five

Fireside Cooking

Build your own fire. Cook over the open flame. Steaks, burgers, foil packets, whatever you bring — there's something primal about dinner you cooked yourself under the stars.

Part of every Loxley stay.

The Morning Ritual

TreeMail

Every morning, fresh pastries appear at the entrance to your rope bridge. No knock. No interruption. Just a quiet delivery before you wake up. It's how the forest says good morning.

Part of every stay.

Every Evening

Stars & S'mores

Graham crackers, chocolate, marshmallows, and a fire you built yourself. The stars do the rest.

Part of every Loxley stay.

The Hidden Chapter

The Letter

On the desk you'll find stationery, a pen, and an envelope. Write a letter — to yourself, to your partner, to the person you want to be a year from now. Seal it. Leave it with us. In twelve months, it arrives at your door.

Stationery in every treehouse. The letter is between you and the future.
